Interactive Cu²⁺ Adsorption Dynamics

Welcome! This interactive visualization shows the adsorption of copper ions (Cu²⁺) at 200 ppm onto different adsorbents over time. If you are not familiar with adsorption, here is a brief explanation:

  • Adsorption is the process where ions or molecules attach to the surface of a solid material (adsorbent).
  • The x-axis represents time in minutes.
  • The y-axis shows the amount of Cu²⁺ adsorbed (qt) in mg per gram of adsorbent.
  • Colored lines correspond to different adsorbents and their adsorption kinetics over time.
  • Rectangles represent the adsorbent beds. They blink when a bed reaches ≥95% of its adsorption capacity.
  • After reaching full saturation, beds need to be regerated.
  • You can control the animation using the “Play” and “Pause” buttons.
